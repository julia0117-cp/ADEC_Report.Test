Which Students Face the Most Barriers?

Difficulty finding information and participating in programs is not evenly distributed — certain demographics consistently report higher friction.

◆
Multiracial students report the steepest climb: 40.2% found it difficult or very difficult to locate ADEC program information — nearly 12 points above the overall average of 28.9%. Asian students (31.0%) and female students (31.5%) also outpaced the average. Alumni reported the highest rate of zero career support (9.2%), and multiracial students followed closely at 9.8%.

◆
Informal networks dominate formal channels. Combined, family/friends/peers and faculty/advisors account for over 58% of students' most valuable guidance — while university-provided career tools reach only 15.1%. Hispanic/Latino students are notably less likely to have used career prep services (39.5% yes vs. 56.8% among Black students), pointing to potential outreach gaps.
